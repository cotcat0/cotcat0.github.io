Ok, so taking Algebra I and Geometry should have taught you the prerequisite material for this.

To recap, the degree of a monomial is the sum of all the exponents of the variables in the monomial.
For example, 3x²y²z³ has a degree of 7 because 3 + 2 + 2 = 7.

A degree of a polynomial is the greatest degree of all its monomials.
For example, if you take f(x) = x³y²z² + x²y²z² + 3x² + 7y², you take each of the degrees of the monomials, find the greatest one, and that is the degree.
The degrees of the monomials are 7, 6, 2, and 2 respectively. The greatest among those is 7, therefore the degree of f(x) is 7.

The leading coefficient of a polynomial is the first coefficient when a polynomial's monomials are written in descending order.

t(x) = 4x² + 6x²y³ + y³

The monomials of this polynomial are written out of order. The degrees of the monomials are 2, 5, and 3 respectively. To list them in descending order would be 5, 3, and 2.

t(x) = 6x²y³ + y³ + 4x²

Now that the polynomial is in standard form, the first coefficient is 6, therefore the leading coefficient is 6.
